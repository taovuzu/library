- Jumping straight into solving a problem based on an idea.  
- Spending **too much time** without making real progress.  
- Getting **stuck in thinking** without a clear direction.  

**Why does this happen?**  
- The **idea might be correct**, but the contestant lacks knowledge of related algorithms to continue.  
- The **idea might be correct**, but insufficient observations were made.  
- The **approach might be correct** (e.g., using DP), but the **specific recurrence is wrong** and needs adjustment.  
- The **approach itself might be incorrect**, requiring a completely different way of thinking.

### **How to Avoid Getting Stuck?**

For example:  
- If asked to **find the minimum X**, consider different approaches:  
  **DP, Greedy, Min Cut, Branch & Bound, or some ad-hoc technique** might work.

- But then, with the right **observation**, a **sparse space optimization** could make it feasible.  
- What seemed impossible **suddenly becomes solvable!**
